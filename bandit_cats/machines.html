Search, or seeking a goal under uncertainty, is a ubiquitous requirement of life. Animals forage for food, territory, and mates. Humans engage in a wide variety of search behaviors, from looking for lost keys, to finding financial opportunities, to seeking the meaning of existence. Moreover, they search in a wide range of spaces, including visual scenes, memory, social networks, information databases, and more abstract environments such as problem spaces associated with cures for disease, product design, and governmental policy. Search in each of these domains involves trade-offs between exploiting known opportunities and exploring for better opportunities elsewhere.
